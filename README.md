# Bank-Churn-Analysis-Dashboard 

**1. Project Title / Headline**
Banking Customer Exit & Behavioral Segmentation
Predicting "Silent Exits" through Geographic and Activity-Based Analysis

**2. Short Description & Purpose**
The goal of this project was to analyze retail banking data to segment customers and predict exit trends. By tracking activity levels and demographic data, I built a system to identify "Silent Exits"—customers who haven't officially closed their accounts but have stopped all transactional behavior.

**3. Tech Stack**
Visualization: Power BI Desktop

Data Modeling: Star Schema (Fact & Dimension Tables)

Analytical Logic: Advanced DAX (Month-over-Month calculations)

Data Prep: SQL (Joins and Table Union)

**4. Data Source**
Kaggle: Utilized a comprehensive Banking Dataset containing 10,000+ customer records across different European regions, including credit scores, balances, and activity status.

**5. Features and Highlights**
Business Problem: The bank saw rising exit rates but lacked a way to see if the issue was regional or based on specific customer habits (like credit card usage).

Goal of Dashboard: To provide a localized "Risk Map" that identifies which branches or regions are losing the most customers and why.

**Key Visuals:**

Activity KPI Cards: Comparing Active vs. Inactive customer ratios.

Regional Heatmaps: Identifying geographical "Hotspots" for churn.

MoM Exit Trends: Tracking the speed of customer exits over a timeline.

**Impact & Insight:**

Regional Anomaly: Isolated a 15% higher churn rate in specific cities, suggesting the need for localized retention offers.

Predictive Behavior: Found that an Inactive Status combined with zero product usage was the strongest predictor of a customer leaving within 30 days.
